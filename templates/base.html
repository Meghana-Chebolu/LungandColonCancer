Lung Cancer

Lung cancer, a leading cause of cancer-related deaths, manifests in the lungs, often linked to smoking or exposure to carcinogens. Symptoms include persistent cough, chest pain, and difficulty breathing. Early detection through screening can improve outcomes, with treatment options including surgery, chemotherapy, and immunotherapy.

Colon Cancer

Colon cancer affects the colon or rectum, typically developing from precancerous polyps. Symptoms include changes in bowel habits, abdominal pain, and blood in stool. Screening, such as colonoscopy, can detect it early, enhancing treatment success with options like surgery, chemotherapy, and targeted therapy.
